Our story

A workshop before it was a website

Anvil & Oak started in a one-room workshop in Dharmapuri in 2014, with two workbenches and a single roll of vegetable-tanned leather. We've grown since — but the workbenches haven't moved.

How it started

From repairing saddles to building a workshop

Meera learned leatherwork repairing saddles for the family farm. When the saddle work slowed down, the skill didn't go anywhere — it turned into bags, then wallets, then a small team of five makers who each sign the pieces they finish.

Today we still cut every pattern by hand, and every order ships from the same workshop where it was made.

What we won't compromise on

Three things we hold the line on

Full-grain only

No bonded or corrected leather, ever — even when it would cut our costs in half.

One maker, one piece

A single craftsperson sees each item from cut to finish, and signs the inside lining.

Repair, not replace

Every Anvil & Oak piece can be sent back to us for restitching or re-conditioning, for life.

Why handmade

Machines are fast. Hands notice things.

A stitching machine doesn't feel a thin spot in the hide before it tears the thread. Our makers do. That's the difference between a bag that lasts a season and one that gets handed down. We make slower, on purpose.

"We don't chase volume. We chase the eighth stitch being as tight as the first."

— Meera Raghunathan, Founder
